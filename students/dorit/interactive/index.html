It was interesting to hear and think about the similarities between AI and humans in that we don't want to be fixed, even if "someone" thinks it's for the better. But this video in itself did not raise particular fears in me. However, it did prompt me to watch another video, "Deadly Truth of General AI?", which, in conjunction with the first, did raise a fear. We tell AI to do something, and it does it the best way it can. This means that in can do it in ways that we can't necessarily predict, which could end up in our destruction. And if we realize it, we can't fix it, because it doesn't want to be fixed. So there.
There's actually nothing I'm too excited about with regard to AI. Especially not from this video. I guess it can potentially do a lot of great things, but since this video didn't talk about it, well, what can I say?
I guess the main question I would have is what do you do, then? If you build a machine that doesn't want to be fixed, but you can't build it perfect in the first try, what do you do? Is it worth messing with it? I'm not a technophobe, but these videos did raise a slight fear of something I never particularly thought about before. I guess that's what all this research is for.
This video wasn't particularly clear to me on my first watch. In fact, without watching the other video, "Deadly Truth of General AI?", I wouldn't have understood much of it.
