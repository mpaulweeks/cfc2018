DON'T MAKE ME THINK!

I found the first few chapters of Steve Krug's, Don't Make Me Think!, to be a smooth read. It was very direct and informative. Not the most exciting thing I've ever read, but hey, not bad. I would have to agree one hundred percent with the focus of the article, which was that people do not like to think when it comes to technology and using web pages. Good design is the result of intuitive clicking on web pages that are designed in a self explanatory manner.

Three main lessons I learned are listed as follows:

  1. Majority of the time, we don't choose the best option when scanning a page, we choose the first reasonable option.
  2. One should design a site that can be easily scanned, not read thoroughly. C'mon, people don't like to read shit!
  3. Reduced "noise" makes a page much easier to grasp. Noise can result from multiple factors, such as
    shouting: when EVERYTHING on the PAGE is asking for the user's ATTENTION
    disorganization: exactly what it sounds like
    clutter: waaaaaaaaaaay too much stuff that makes it hard to read
